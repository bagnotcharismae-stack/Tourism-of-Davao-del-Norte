INDIGENOUS GROUPS

Preserving the Rich Cultural Heritage of Davao del Norte

Davao del Norte is a culturally rich province home to various indigenous communities, primarily the Ata-Manobo, Dibabawon, and Mansaka peoples. These groups are the ancestral guardians of the province's highland frontiers, particularly in the mountainous landscapes of Talaingod and Kapalong. Their heritage is preserved through intricate weaving traditions, vibrant beadwork, and sacred rituals that honor their deep connection to the land and the forest. Visitors can experience this living history through local cultural presentations and festivals, which showcase traditional music, dances, and a sustainable way of life that remains a vital part of the province's identity.

TOP INDIGENOUS GROUPS

Ata-Manobo Tribe

The Ata-Manobo are known for their intricate weaving, forest-based livelihoods, and unique cultural rituals that have been passed down through generations.

Kalagan Tribe

The Kalagan people are recognized for their vibrant festivals, traditional music, and close-knit community life that reflects their rich heritage.

Bagobo Tribe

The Bagobo Tribe is famous for their traditional dances, colorful attire, and elaborate rituals that celebrate life, nature, and spirituality.

Mandaya Tribe

The Mandaya Tribe is known for their exquisite embroidery, cultural storytelling, and preservation of ancestral customs within their communities.
