LAKES & RIVERS

Waterways that Sustain Life and Nature

Davao del Norte is defined by its abundant water resources, featuring expansive river systems and serene inland bodies of water that are vital to both its agriculture and tourism. The province is crisscrossed by major waterways like the Libuganon River and the Tibal-og River, which not only provide essential irrigation for the vast banana plantations but also offer peaceful spots for nature viewing and local recreation. In the highland areas and coastal plains, these waters often lead to hidden gems and wetlands that support rich biodiversity. Whether it is the calm flow of a river winding through the countryside or the still beauty of small inland lakes, these waters highlight the province's lush, fertile environment and its deep connection to the natural world.

TOP LAKES & RIVERS IN DAVAO DEL NORTE

Taguibo River

A vital river for agriculture and local communities, supporting irrigation and daily water needs.

Matina River

Supports local irrigation and fishing, contributing to the livelihood of nearby communities.

Lake Buluan

A small freshwater lake known for fishing and its scenic surroundings.
