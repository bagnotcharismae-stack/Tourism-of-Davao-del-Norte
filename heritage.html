HERITAGE SITES

Discover the Rich History of Davao del Norte

Davao del Norte is home to several heritage sites and cultural landmarks that preserve the province's history and spiritual identity. Among the most prominent is the Davao del Norte Sports and Tourism Complex, which stands as a modern symbol of the province’s unity and its successful hosting of national milestones. The New Tagum City Hall is another architectural landmark, celebrated for its grandiose, futuristic design that reflects the province’s progressive vision. For those seeking historical and spiritual depth, the Christ the King Cathedral in Tagum—home to the world's largest wooden rosary—serves as a major pilgrimage site. These locations, along with the preserved ancestral domains of the Ata-Manobo in the highlands, offer a meaningful look into the blend of contemporary achievement and deep-rooted traditions that define the region.

TOP HERITAGE SITES

D’ Bone Collector Museum

A unique museum showcasing preserved skeletal remains of marine and land animals, offering educational insight into biodiversity and wildlife conservation.

Museo Dabawenyo

A government-run museum dedicated to preserving Davao’s history, indigenous heritage, and significant events that shaped the city. Exhibits include cultural artifacts from different Mindanaoan tribes.

The Upside Down Museum

A fun and interactive museum featuring optical illusion displays and gravity-defying rooms. It’s a great place for families and tourists looking for a unique experience.
